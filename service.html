OUR WORKS

One Love Food Kitchen
Intervention Issue:

Hunger

The One Love Food Kitchen was our first intervention, the intervention was a response to the impact of the COVID-I9 impact on poor and vulnerable households and community, From October 1, 2020 to October 30, 2020, PCAI cooked food ranging from rice, beans and potato and served to vulnerable people in three slum communities in Port-Harcourt. Breakfast consisting of bread, milk and milo was served to children who had resumed school as at when the interventions was going on.

Total No of Beneficiaries:

12

Communities served:

– Fe Polo, Ibimina Polo, Amagboribipifie Polo

Project status:

Ongoing

Volunteer Induction Training
Intervention Issue:

Volunteer Capacity Development

The volunteer induction training is a two-day workshop for intending volunteers who have signed up to be volunteers by completing the expression of interest form. The training was facilitated by experienced and seasoned resource person with vast experience in leadership, social work, volunteering. Topics covered includes; volunteering, overview of PCAI, community engagement, communication, code of conduct for PCAI Volunteers.

Total No of Beneficiaries:

170 volunteers

Communities served:

Africa Builders Community

Community-Needs Assessment Meeting
Intervention Issue:

Community development plan

The Community Needs Assessment and Priority setting meeting was organized as a strategy for community participation in their development proces. The meeting had community members from the men, women, youths and the leadership gathered. each group deliberated on what their needs where and prioritized them. Each group presented their needs and there was harmonization of the needs and a voting processes was conducted after which the priority list of community needs was arrived at.

Total No of Beneficiaries:

12

Communities served:

8

Project status:

Completed

Back To School
Intervention Issue:

Out-of-school-children

The BSC Campaign is an intervention targeted at out-of-school-children, the intervention seeks to support indigent children whose parents can’t afford to send to school go to school starting with a six months remedial program. The intervention identified 124 children during the identification exercise, 69 turned up for classes when we started the 6-month remedial program and 50 completed the remedial program.

Total No of Beneficiaries:

69

Communities served:

Fe Polo

Project status:

Ongoing

Damiete Horsfall ICT Resource Center
Intervention Issue:

STEM/ICT EDUCATION

The DH-ICT-RC is a 30-computer systems facility at the PCAI office complex, established for the teaching/learning of STEM/ICT based skills to indigent people from the communities we currently work in and those we will work in, in the future. The center is also designed to have paid classes which will serve as a means of revenue mobilization.
